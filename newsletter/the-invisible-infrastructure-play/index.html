THE INVISIBLE INFRASTRUCTURE PLAY

Infrastructure

Is Ethereum bleeding value or executing the most brilliant infrastructure strategy in crypto?

While critics wring their hands over L2s “stealing” Ethereum’s value, institutions bet billions that Ethereum’s approach is genius, not weakness.

BlackRock’s Robert Mitchnick put it bluntly: “Many of the fears around Ethereum’s future are overdone.”

He’s right. And here’s why.

The Great Misunderstanding

Critics see Ethereum’s numbers and panic.

Arbitrum: $1.1M revenue, $55K costs = $1M+ profit. Base: $15M revenue, $1.6M costs = $13M+ profit.

“Those profits should go to ETH holders!” they cry.

This perspective fundamentally misunderstands what Ethereum is building.

Ethereum isn’t trying to extract maximum value. That’s by design. It’s the strategy, not a bug.

The Permissionless Infrastructure Pattern

History’s most transformative technologies follow a pattern:

TCP/IP: Captured virtually zero value. Enabled trillions in commerce.

Linux: Minimal direct revenue. Powers 96% of servers, 100% of supercomputers, 73% of smartphones.

They won by being:

Permissionless - no gatekeepers

Neutral - no controlling interests

Open-universal standards

Resilient - distributed architecture

Their impact was massive, but their value capture was minimal.

Ethereum’s Revolutionary Break

Ethereum is the first permissionless infrastructure with built-in value capture:

Native asset (ETH) required for all network activity

Security model generating sustainable yield through staking

Fee-burning mechanism that reduces supply as usage increases

Settlement role for an entire ecosystem of applications

This innovation solves the paradox that plagued previous open infrastructure.

The Economic No-Brainer

The numbers are staggering.

If Arbitrum operated as an independent chain, its costs would increase 24-65x.

From $660K annually to $16-43M.

Celo saw this reality and “came home” to Ethereum, saving up to $25M annually in security costs alone.

Independent L1s face a brutal economic reality:

4-6% of tokens issued just for security

No retained profit margin

Limited reinvestment ability

Security costs that don’t decrease with scale

Building on Ethereum gives you:

World-class security at a fraction of the cost

Immediate access to liquidity and users

Thousands of the world’s best blockchain developers continuously improving your infrastructure

It’s not even close.

The Value Extraction vs. Value Distribution Philosophy

Centralized alternatives like BSC (21 validators) and Tron (27 validators) pursue maximum value extraction for their operators.

Their entire design philosophy: control the platform, extract the fees.

Ethereum’s approach? Become an invisible infrastructure that empowers thousands of projects to flourish independently.

For enterprises, using BSC or Tron means accepting you’re on someone else’s platform—the blockchain equivalent of building your business on Facebook, where the platform owner changes rules at will.

Ethereum offers neutral ground no single entity controls.

What Financial Giants See

Major financial institutions aren’t fooled by the “bleeding value” narrative.

BlackRock’s tokenized fund on Ethereum grew to $1.7B with 361% monthly growth, while versions on other chains stagnated.

Deutsche Bank is building an Ethereum L2 with zkSync technology, creating a “public but permissioned” network for institutional finance.

They chose Ethereum for reasons that alternative chains simply cannot match:

100% uptime since 2015

Security backed by billions in staked ETH

Geopolitical neutrality

Censorship resistance

Global developer ecosystem

These institutions did their homework. They’re betting on Ethereum’s approach because it works.

The Strategic Flywheel

Ethereum’s strategy creates a powerful flywheel effect:

L2s make Ethereum accessible to more users and developers

More activity increases demand for ETH (for security, gas, staking)

Higher ETH value strengthens network security

Stronger security attracts more institutions and enterprises

Institutional adoption drives more L2 development

This virtuous cycle is already spinning. Every new L2, every institutional adoption, every migrating project accelerates it.

The Long Game

The “bleeding value” critique focuses on short-term fee distribution while missing the strategic brilliance.

Ethereum is deliberately seeding an ecosystem that will circle back to reinforce its dominance.

By positioning itself as the secure settlement layer for a diverse ecosystem of L2 solutions, Ethereum becomes the TCP/IP of crypto—the indispensable base layer everyone relies on.

But unlike TCP/IP, Ethereum captures value through ETH, staking yield, and fee burning.

The Key Insight

Most blockchains are trying to become the next Visa or Mastercard—extracting maximum fees from each transaction.

Ethereum is becoming the settlement layer for a new financial system.

Like TCP/IP became the backbone of the internet.

Like Linux became the foundation for most computing.

But with one crucial difference: built-in economic alignment.

What This Means For You

For developers: Building on Ethereum (via L2s) gives you maximum security at minimum cost, plus access to the deepest liquidity and talent pool.

For investors: Look beyond short-term fee metrics. Ethereum’s strategic positioning creates multiple long-term value drivers through ETH’s essential role in the ecosystem.

For enterprises: Ethereum offers unique qualities that centralized alternatives cannot match—neutrality, resilience, and security no single entity can compromise.

The next time someone claims Ethereum is “bleeding value,” ask them if they think TCP/IP was a failure because it didn’t charge per packet.

Ethereum isn’t bleeding value. It’s strategically seeding an ecosystem that makes it indispensable.

That’s not a weakness. That’s genius.
